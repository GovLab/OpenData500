Open Data 100 México

As part of its Open Data policy, the Federal Government of Mexico is working in partnership with The GovLab at New York University to conduct the Open Data 100 Mexico. This is the first study of Mexican companies and NGO's that use open government data to generate new business, develop new products and services, and create social value.

More Information

Study Objectives

  • Provide a basis for assessing the economic and social value of open government data
  • Stimulate the development of new companies working with Open Data
  • Facilitate dialogue between the government and the private sector on how to improve the usefulness of open government data

Strategy

The publishing process of government public data as Open Data is a process of gradual change. Therefore, this study will seek companies / organizations from various sectors, small, medium and large, for-profit, nonprofit, or governmental which use public data as an integral part of their business.

This includes any information or statistics from government agencies. While most companies exhibited at the initiative of Open Data 100 Mexico working with federal open data, we also have interest in companies that work with state or municipal data, on the understanding that the management of their data can be expanded to regional or national level.

Company Identification

The team collected data Mexico Open 100 list of 100 companies and NGOs through:

  1. communication campaigns
  2. recommendations of experts and professional organizations
  3. further investigation

The results of the study will culminate in an online platform at www.opendata500.com/mx, where various companies and their work with open government data in Mexico will be highlighted.

What the Study is Not

Open Data 100 Mexico is not a ranking of the companies in terms of quality or performance. It covers companies and entities of different sizes and categories, using various kinds of data. The Open Data 100 Mexico is not a competition, but an attempt to give a broad, inclusive view of the field. The study also does not provide a random sample for definitive statistical analysis. Since this is the first thorough scan of companies and entities in the field, it is not yet possible to determine the exact landscape of open data companies and entities.
